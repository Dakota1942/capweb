Keeping You & Your Devices Safe

Security awareness is used in organizations to educate people on cybersecurity and reduce some of the risk that comes from accidents or people that have bad intentions. It is important to be aware of threats and how they could affect you and others at the university. An accident could be as simple as someone clicking on a bad link in an email and entering their credentials when it asks. This could give a hacker the ability to gain access to sensitive information. Taking a little time to learn about security threats and how to reduce risk, will help to mitigate the risk to everyone connected to the university.

Weak Passwords

Simple variations of words/phrases (e.g., "p@ssword", "pa$$w0rd") and personal information like birthdays and pet names are easily guessed by hackers. Strong passwords are at the very least 12 characters long and use a mixture of numbers, upper and lowercase letters, and special characters.

MFA

Why multi-factor authentication? Your accounts are protected by requiring more than just a password to log in. Multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security, typically a unique code texted to you or generated by an app, making it impossible for hackers to gain access.

Phishing Mail

These messages are convincing. They can include "URGENT!" and "ALERT!" in the subject line. You can sniff out a sketchy email if generic greetings like "Dear User/Customer" are used. These messages tend to request personal information, and have awkward phrasing and grammatical errors.

Password Managers

Password managers can be helpful for storing multiple passwords and protecting them. Bitwarden has a free tier password manager that allows users to store multiple passwords across several devices. Follow the link to download bitwarden: https://bitwarden.com/download.
